Varicocele

A Leading Cause of Male Infertility

A varicocele is an enlargement of the veins inside the scrotum — similar to a varicose vein in the leg. It is one of the most common and most treatable causes of low sperm count and male infertility. Many men have no symptoms at all and only discover it during a fertility check-up, while others notice a dull ache or a "bag of worms" feeling above the testicle, especially after standing for long periods.

Common Signs

  • Dull, dragging ache in the scrotum
  • Swelling or visible enlarged veins
  • Discomfort that worsens through the day
  • Difficulty conceiving / low sperm count
  • Mild shrinkage of the affected testicle

Diagnosis & Treatment

  • Physical exam & scrotal Doppler ultrasound
  • Semen analysis to assess fertility impact
  • Observation for mild, painless cases
  • Varicocelectomy (microsurgical repair)
  • Often improves sperm count & pain relief

When to see a doctor: if you feel a scrotal lump, ongoing ache, or are struggling to conceive, an evaluation is worthwhile — varicocele is correctable and early treatment protects fertility.

Erectile Dysfunction (ED)

Treatable at Almost Any Age

Erectile dysfunction means difficulty getting or keeping an erection firm enough for satisfying intercourse. It is extremely common and rarely "just in the mind" — in most men there is a real, identifiable cause such as poor blood flow, diabetes, hormonal imbalance, stress, or medication side effects. The good news: ED is one of the most successfully treated conditions in andrology.

Possible Causes

  • Diabetes & high blood pressure
  • Reduced penile blood flow
  • Low testosterone / hormonal issues
  • Stress, anxiety & relationship strain
  • Smoking, obesity & certain medicines

Treatment Options

  • Root-cause testing (hormones, sugar, vascular)
  • Oral medication where appropriate
  • Lifestyle & cardiovascular optimisation
  • Counselling for performance anxiety
  • Advanced therapies for resistant cases

Important: ED can be an early warning sign of heart disease or diabetes. Treating it properly is about your overall health — not just performance.

Premature Ejaculation (PE)

The Most Common Sexual Complaint in Men

Premature ejaculation is when ejaculation happens sooner than a man or his partner would like — often within a minute or before he wishes. It affects men of every age and is highly treatable with the right combination of behavioural techniques, topical agents, and medication when needed. There is no reason to feel embarrassed: it is a medical condition, not a character flaw.

Contributing Factors

  • Performance anxiety & stress
  • Over-sensitivity or early conditioning
  • Hormonal or serotonin imbalance
  • Underlying ED or prostatitis
  • Relationship pressure

How It Is Managed

  • Behavioural training (start–stop, squeeze)
  • Topical desensitising agents
  • Targeted oral medication when indicated
  • Treating any linked ED together
  • Counselling for confidence & control

Reassurance: most men see clear improvement with a structured plan. Treating PE and ED together gives the best long-term results.

Male Infertility & Low Sperm Count

When Conceiving Is Taking Too Long

In roughly half of couples struggling to conceive, a male factor is involved. Male infertility is usually painless and has no outward symptoms — which is why a simple semen analysis is so important. Causes range from varicocele and hormonal imbalance to infection, lifestyle, and genetic factors. Many of these are correctable, and a clear plan can dramatically improve the chance of natural conception.

Common Causes

  • Varicocele (very common & treatable)
  • Low sperm count or poor motility
  • Hormonal imbalance (low testosterone, FSH/LH)
  • Infection or blockage
  • Smoking, heat, weight & lifestyle

Evaluation & Care

  • Semen analysis & hormone profile
  • Scrotal ultrasound where needed
  • Treat the underlying cause (e.g. varicocele)
  • Lifestyle & nutritional optimisation
  • Referral for assisted options if required

Tip for couples: evaluating the man first is quick, inexpensive, and non-invasive — start there before more complex testing.

Low Testosterone & Hormonal Imbalance

Energy, Mood, Drive & Fertility

Testosterone is the key male hormone behind sex drive, energy, muscle, mood, and sperm production. When it drops too low — sometimes called "low T" or andropause — men can feel tired, low, and notice reduced libido and performance. A simple blood test confirms it, and treatment is tailored carefully to the cause rather than handed out as a one-size-fits-all injection.

Symptoms to Watch

  • Low sex drive & weak erections
  • Constant fatigue & low mood
  • Loss of muscle, gain in belly fat
  • Poor concentration & motivation
  • Reduced fertility

Assessment & Treatment

  • Morning testosterone & full hormone panel
  • Check thyroid, prolactin & sugar
  • Treat the cause, not just the number
  • Carefully monitored hormone therapy
  • Fertility-safe options for younger men

Caution: never self-medicate with testosterone — unsupervised use can shrink the testes and reduce fertility. Proper monitoring matters.

Prostate Health & BPH

Common After 45 — Very Manageable

The prostate gland naturally enlarges with age (benign prostatic hyperplasia, or BPH), which can press on the urinary tube and cause bothersome urinary symptoms. Prostatitis (inflammation/infection) can also cause pain and burning. Most prostate concerns are not cancer, but proper screening gives peace of mind and prevents complications.

Urinary Symptoms

  • Weak or interrupted urine flow
  • Frequent urination, especially at night
  • Urgency or difficulty starting
  • Feeling the bladder isn't empty
  • Burning or pelvic discomfort

Care Pathway

  • Symptom score, exam & PSA when indicated
  • Ultrasound & flow assessment
  • Medication to relax/shrink the prostate
  • Treatment of prostatitis/infection
  • Surgical referral for advanced BPH

Don't ignore it: untreated BPH can lead to infections and bladder damage. Early review keeps treatment simple.

Urethral Stricture Disease

A Special Area of Focus

A urethral stricture is a narrowing of the urine tube (urethra), usually caused by previous infection, injury, or instrumentation. It slows the urine stream and, if neglected, can cause infections, bladder strain, and even kidney problems. Dr. Muhammad Zeeshan Ahmad Raza has a particular interest in diagnosing and managing stricture disease.

Warning Signs

  • Thin, weak or splitting urine stream
  • Straining to pass urine
  • Incomplete emptying & dribbling
  • Recurrent urinary infections
  • Pain or burning on passing urine

Diagnosis & Options

  • Uroflowmetry & urethral imaging
  • Cystoscopy to locate the narrowing
  • Dilatation for selected cases
  • Optical internal urethrotomy
  • Urethroplasty for long-term cure

Key point: a weak urine stream in a younger man is not normal — it deserves proper assessment rather than repeated, temporary fixes.

Penile Health, Curvature & Augmentation

Structure, Function & Confidence

Concerns about penile shape, curvature (Peyronie's disease), tightness of the foreskin, or size are common and valid reasons to see an andrologist. Some conditions cause painful erections or affect function; others are mainly about confidence. A professional, judgment-free assessment separates what genuinely needs treatment from what does not.

Concerns Covered

  • Penile curvature / Peyronie's disease
  • Painful erections or palpable plaque
  • Foreskin problems (phimosis)
  • Concerns about size or appearance
  • Post-injury or post-surgical changes

Approach

  • Honest assessment of what is normal
  • Medical treatment for early Peyronie's
  • Counselling for body-image concerns
  • Surgical referral when truly indicated
  • Realistic, evidence-based guidance

Honesty first: beware of unproven creams, devices, and "guaranteed" augmentation claims. Always seek qualified medical advice.

Nightfall, Sexual Weakness & Performance Anxiety

Separating Myth From Medicine

Many men worry about nightfall (wet dreams), "sexual weakness," or loss of timing and confidence — often after reading misleading information or trying unregulated remedies. In reality, occasional nightfall is completely normal and harmless. Genuine problems with desire, erections, or stamina have real medical explanations and real solutions — not shame-based ones.

What Men Ask About

  • Frequent nightfall & whether it's harmful
  • Loss of stamina or desire
  • Confidence & performance anxiety
  • Effects of past habits or myths
  • Fatigue blamed on "weakness"

The Real Answers

  • Reassurance: nightfall is normal
  • Check hormones, sugar & general health
  • Treat any real ED, PE or low-T
  • Stop harmful, unregulated "tonics"
  • Evidence-based advice, not fear

Beware: roadside "sex tonics" and quack treatments can be dangerous. A proper consultation is safer, cheaper, and actually works.

Married Life & Sexual Wellness

For Couples, Newlyweds & Long-Term Partners

A healthy intimate relationship is part of a happy marriage — and difficulties are far more common than couples realise. Whether it is performance worry on the wedding night, mismatched desire, pain, difficulty conceiving, or simply a lack of reliable information, these issues are medical and relational, not taboo. Confidential guidance can save couples years of needless stress.

Common Married-Life Concerns

  • Wedding-night anxiety & first-time worries
  • Erectile or timing difficulties
  • Low or mismatched desire
  • Pain or difficulty with intercourse
  • Trouble conceiving as a couple

How We Help

  • Private, judgment-free consultation
  • Accurate education for both partners
  • Treat medical causes (ED, PE, low-T)
  • Fertility guidance for couples
  • Practical, respectful counselling

You are not alone: thousands of couples face these concerns. Asking for help early is a sign of maturity, not weakness.

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions Men Often Ask

Straight answers to the questions patients are sometimes too shy to ask out loud.

Is everything I discuss kept confidential?
Absolutely. Every consultation is completely private. Your information is never shared with anyone, and you can discuss sensitive matters without any judgment.
Can a varicocele really affect my fertility?
Yes. Varicocele is one of the most common — and most correctable — causes of low sperm count. Many men improve their fertility after proper diagnosis and treatment, so it is well worth checking if you are struggling to conceive.
Is erectile dysfunction a normal part of getting older?
While ED becomes more common with age, it is never something you simply have to accept. In most men there is a treatable cause such as poor blood flow, diabetes, stress, or low testosterone. It can also be an early warning sign of heart disease, so it is worth addressing properly.
Is nightfall (wet dreams) harmful or a sign of weakness?
No. Occasional nightfall is completely normal and harmless for men of all ages. Much of the worry around it comes from myths and misinformation. If you have genuine concerns about desire, stamina, or performance, those have real medical explanations and solutions.
Should I take testosterone or "sex tonics" I can buy without prescription?
Please don't. Unsupervised testosterone can shrink the testes and harm fertility, and roadside "tonics" can be genuinely dangerous. Any hormone treatment should be guided by blood tests and proper monitoring.
I'm getting married soon and feel anxious — can you help?
Yes, this is one of the most common reasons younger men book a consultation. A short, confidential visit for accurate information and reassurance — and treatment of any real medical issue — can make a big difference to confidence.
Do you offer online consultations?
Yes. Secure online video consultations are available by appointment for Rs. 2,500, so you can get expert advice privately from anywhere in Pakistan.
What is a urethral stricture and how do I know if I have one?
A urethral stricture is a narrowing of the urine tube that causes a thin or weak stream, straining, and recurrent infections. A weak urine stream — especially in a younger man — is not normal and should be assessed rather than ignored.
